I am a Cinema Arts student at Central Michigan University, currently pursuing a Bachelor of Fine Arts with a focus on storytelling through visual media. As an aspiring screenwriter, cinematographer, and director, I am passionate about crafting compelling narratives and bringing them to life through film, television, and broadcast production.

My academic experience includes coursework in film history, cinematography, video production, photography, animation, audio production, and mixed media arts—providing me with a well-rounded foundation in both the creative and technical aspects of filmmaking. Beyond the classroom, I am actively involved in the CMU Film Society and Spectrum Club, where I collaborate with peers and contribute to a creative community.

I have also gained hands-on industry experience through volunteer work with Midland Community Television, where I assist with camera operation and technical production. My dedication to cinema extends outside of production, as I regularly attend major film festivals, including the Sundance Film Festival and the Toronto International Film Festival, to stay engaged with contemporary filmmaking and global storytelling trends.

In addition to my work in film, I have an extensive background in music, with over a decade of classical piano training and experience playing guitar, cello, and violin. This musical foundation informs my creative approach and deepens my understanding of rhythm, tone, and emotion in visual storytelling.

Driven by a love for cinema, music, and travel, I am eager to contribute to the film, television, or broadcast industries and continue growing as a visual storyteller.
